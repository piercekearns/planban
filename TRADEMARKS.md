# Planban Trademark Guidelines

Planban is open-source software, but the Planban name, logo, wordmark, domain,
social handles, and official release channels identify the official Planban
project and are not granted under the MIT license.

You may:

- refer to the official project as Planban;
- say that your project is built with, compatible with, or forked from Planban;
- use the Planban name in issue reports, pull requests, tutorials, reviews, and
  other ordinary descriptive references.

You may not, without written permission:

- present a fork, plugin, service, package, or marketplace listing as the official
  Planban project;
- use the Planban logo, wordmark, or confusingly similar branding for a fork or
  derivative product;
- imply endorsement, sponsorship, or affiliation by Planban or Pierce Kearns;
- use the Planban name for a hosted service, commercial offering, or product name
  in a way that could confuse users about who operates it.

Suggested naming for forks and integrations:

- `your-project for Planban`
- `Planban integration for your-project`
- `your-project, forked from Planban`

Avoid names like:

- `Planban Pro`
- `Planban Cloud`
- `Official Planban`
- `Planban for Teams`

These guidelines are intentionally lightweight. They are meant to let people fork,
study, improve, and integrate with Planban while keeping the official project and
future hosted services recognizable and trustworthy.
